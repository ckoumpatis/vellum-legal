Section 4

Data Collection Methodology and Limitations

Our VEIL (Visibility and Extraction Intelligence Layer) dataset is generated through the following process:

  • A set of queries reflecting natural language search behaviour by US-based property owners, landlords, and real estate investors is submitted nightly to AI platform APIs.
  • AI-generated responses are recorded verbatim, timestamped, and stored in structured JSON format.
  • Citation presence, position, sentiment, and competitive context are extracted programmatically from each response.
  • All data is archived to a private version-controlled repository with commit timestamps constituting the primary provenance record.
Our data reflects the state of AI platform responses at the time of each nightly run. It does not reflect real-time AI behaviour, user-facing interfaces, or any AI outputs generated outside of our specific API query methodology.

The following limitations apply to all data and findings produced by Vellum Strategic Advisory:

  • AI model drift. AI platforms update their underlying models without notice. A finding that is accurate on a given date may not reflect the platform's behaviour on a subsequent date. We log model versions and flag model events in our reporting.
  • Platform coverage. We monitor a defined set of platforms. We do not monitor all AI platforms, all interfaces, or all possible query formulations. Absence of a finding in our data does not constitute evidence of absence in the broader AI landscape.
  • Geographic scope. All queries are submitted from infrastructure operating in the United States market context. Results may differ for queries submitted from other geographic locations. Our data is not representative of non-US markets.
  • Query selection. Our query set reflects our professional judgment of representative buyer language. It does not constitute a statistically exhaustive sample of all possible queries a buyer might submit.
  • Perplexity suspension. As of April 2026, Perplexity AI data is excluded from our dataset due to confirmed geographic contamination affecting query results from non-US IP addresses. This suspension is disclosed in all reports covering any period during which Perplexity was active in our methodology.
  • Data gaps. Any period in which our nightly runner experienced technical failure is documented as a continuity event and disclosed in all reports covering that date range.
  • No live data. Our deliverables reflect historical dataset snapshots. We do not provide live or real-time AI monitoring unless explicitly contracted.
